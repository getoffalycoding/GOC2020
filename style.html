Style Guide

This page contains information regarding typography usage and brand colours.

Typography

For headings, we use Montserrat, a geometric sans-serif typeface. We generally use the 500 or 600 weight depending on the document or webpage display. 700-900 is rarely used in documents but may be used in webpages as page titles. In cases where Montserrat might not be available for use, Open Sans 700 or Raleway may be used as a replacement. Avenir Next Pro Light or Proxima Nova may be used as a replacement if the case in question relates to a document, spreadsheet or PowerPoint presentation. 

For subtitles or second headings we use Open Sans, a humanist sans serif typeface. We generally use the 400 or 500 weight depending on the document or webpage display. In cases where Montserrat might not be available for use, Roboto or Lato may be used as a replacement. Avenir Next Pro Light or Proxima Nova may be used as a replacement if the case in question relates to a document, spreadsheet or PowerPoint presentation. 

For the main text we use Inter. Inter is a sans-serif font family carefully crafted & designed for computer screens. We generally use the 400 weight depending on the document or webpage display. In cases where Inter might not be available for use, Muli or Open Sans may be used as a replacement. Avenir Next Pro or Proxima Nova may be used as a replacement if the case in question relates to a document, spreadsheet or PowerPoint presentation. 

Montserrat, Open Sans, Raleway, Roboto, Lato, Inter and Muli are open source and available from Google Fonts. Avenir Next Pro and Proxima Nova are not open source font and are paid-for fonts. Inter, Montserrat, Raleway, Lato and Muli are released under the Open Font License. Open Sans and Roboto is released under the Apache License, Version 2.0.
